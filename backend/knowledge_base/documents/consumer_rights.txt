=== CONSUMER PROTECTION AND MRP RULES IN INDIA ===

1. MAXIMUM RETAIL PRICE (MRP) RULES
- No shopkeeper or retailer can charge more than the Maximum Retail Price (MRP) printed on the product packaging.
- Charging above MRP is a violation of the Legal Metrology Act, 2009.
- Punishment for overcharging (First offense): Fine up to ₹25,000.
- Punishment for overcharging (Subsequent offenses): Fine up to ₹50,000 or imprisonment up to 6 months.

2. CONSUMER PROTECTION ACT, 2019
- Right to Safety: Protection against marketed goods and services that are hazardous to life and property.
- Right to be Informed: The right to be informed about the quality, quantity, potency, purity, standard, and price of goods or services.
- Right to Choose: Assured access to a variety of goods or or services at competitive prices.
- Right to be Heard: Consumer's interests will receive due consideration at appropriate forums.
- Right to Redressal: The right to seek redressal against unfair trade practices or unscrupulous exploitation of consumers.

3. HOW TO FILE A COMPLAINT
- National Consumer Helpline (NCH): Call 1915 or SMS to 8800001915.
- Online Portal: Register complaints on the 'e-Daakhil' portal (edaakhil.nic.in).
- District Consumer Commission: For claims up to ₹50 Lakh.
- State Consumer Commission: For claims between ₹50 Lakh and ₹2 Crore.
- National Consumer Commission: For claims above ₹2 Crore.

4. DUAL MRP POLICY
- It is illegal for a company to have different MRPs for the same product in different locations (e.g., at an airport vs a normal shop) unless specifically allowed for different packaging or weight.

5. GST AND MRP
- The MRP is inclusive of all taxes, including GST. A shopkeeper cannot add GST on top of the MRP.
