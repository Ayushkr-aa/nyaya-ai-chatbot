=== eCOURTS SERVICES ===

The eCourts Mission Mode Project is a pan-India project for computerization of district and subordinate courts.

How to check case status on eCourts:
1. Visit https://ecourts.gov.in
2. Click on "Case Status"
3. Choose search method: by CNR number, party name, filing number, case number, FIR number, or advocate name
4. CNR (Case Number Record) is a unique 16-digit number assigned to each case
5. Enter required details and click Search
6. View case details including: next hearing date, case history, orders, judgments

eCourts Services Portal: https://services.ecourts.gov.in
eCourts Mobile App: Available on Google Play Store and Apple App Store
Services available on eCourts:
- Case status tracking by CNR, party name, advocate name, filing number
- Cause list viewing (daily list of cases to be heard)
- Download of court orders and judgments
- Online court fee payment
- Case history and hearing schedule
- District Court and High Court case tracking

=== FAST TRACK COURTS (FTCs) ===

Fast Track Courts were established to ensure speedy disposal of long-pending cases.
Key Facts:
- Over 800 Fast Track Courts operational across India
- Prioritize cases pending for 5+ years
- Handle heinous crimes and cases involving senior citizens
- POCSO Fast Track Courts: 408 exclusive POCSO courts functioning nationally with target disposal within 1 year
- Established under the 14th Finance Commission recommendations
- Currently funded under the Centrally Sponsored Scheme
Website: https://doj.gov.in/fast-track-courts

POCSO Courts specifically handle cases under the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012 (POCSO Act). These courts:
- Conduct child-friendly proceedings
- Record testimony of child victims via video conferencing
- Complete trial within one year
- Provide victim compensation

=== TELE LAW SERVICES ===

Tele Law connects marginalized citizens with empanelled lawyers via video conferencing at Common Service Centres (CSCs).

How Tele Law works:
1. Visit your nearest CSC (Common Service Centre)
2. Register for Tele Law service (free of cost)
3. Get connected to a Panel Lawyer via video call
4. Receive FREE pre-litigation legal advice on any legal matter
5. If needed, connect with a lawyer for further assistance

Eligible beneficiaries:
- SC/ST communities
- Women (especially domestic violence victims)
- BPL (Below Poverty Line) cardholders
- Persons with disabilities
- Senior citizens
- Minorities
- Victims of trafficking and sexual offences

Tele Law Portal: https://tele-law.in
CSC Locator: https://locator.csc.gov.in
Helpline: 1800-180-6270 (toll-free)

As of 2026, Tele Law has provided legal advice to over 50 lakh citizens across India.

=== NATIONAL JUDICIAL DATA GRID (NJDG) ===

NJDG is a real-time database of cases filed and disposed across all courts in India.
What you can find on NJDG:
- Total pending cases (District and Taluka Courts)
- Age-wise pendency (0-1 year, 1-3 years, 3-5 years, 5+ years)
- State-wise, court-wise, and judge-wise statistics
- Civil vs Criminal case breakdowns
- Disposal rates and institution rates

Key Statistics (approximate):
- ~4.4 crore cases pending across all courts
- Criminal cases: ~2.8 crore
- Civil cases: ~1.6 crore
- Cases pending for more than 5 years: significant proportion in several states

Access NJDG: https://njdg.ecourts.gov.in
Updated daily from integrated court servers across India.

=== GRAM NYAYALAYAS (VILLAGE COURTS) ===

Established under the Gram Nyayalayas Act, 2008 to bring justice to the doorstep of rural citizens.

Features:
- Mobile courts — can sit at any place within their jurisdiction
- Handle both civil and criminal matters
- Aim for disposal within 6 months
- Use conciliation and plea bargaining
- Simplified procedures — no lawyers required (self-representation allowed)
- No court fees for certain categories

Jurisdiction:
- Civil disputes up to Rs. 1 lakh
- Minor criminal offences (Schedule I and II of the Act)
- Land disputes, family disputes, small claims

Current Status: ~500+ Gram Nyayalayas notified across India
Website: https://doj.gov.in/gram-nyayalayas

=== LIVE STREAMING OF COURT PROCEEDINGS ===

Several High Courts and the Supreme Court now live-stream proceedings for transparency.

Supreme Court Live Stream:
- YouTube: Supreme Court of India channel
- Only Constitution Bench hearings and cases of public importance are streamed
- Available during court working hours (10:30 AM - 4:30 PM)

High Courts with live streaming:
- Gujarat High Court
- Karnataka High Court
- Orissa High Court
- Jharkhand High Court
- Patna High Court
- And several others

Rules:
- Only designated courtrooms are streamed
- Audio/video recording by viewers is strictly prohibited
- Subject to guidelines issued by the E-Committee of the Supreme Court
- Part of the e-Courts Phase III project

=== ONLINE FINE PAYMENT ===

Steps to pay court fines online:
1. Visit https://ecourts.gov.in/ecourts_home/
2. Select "Payment of Court Fees"
3. Enter your state and court details
4. Enter case/order number
5. View fine amount
6. Pay via UPI / Net Banking / Debit Card / Credit Card

Payment modes accepted:
- UPI (PhonePe, Google Pay, Paytm)
- Net Banking
- Debit/Credit Card
- NEFT/RTGS

For traffic challans: https://echallan.parivahan.gov.in

=== SUPREME COURT OF INDIA ===

Basic Information:
- Established: 28 January 1950
- Location: Tilak Marg, New Delhi
- Judges: 1 Chief Justice + up to 33 other Judges
- Current CJI: As appointed by the President

Jurisdiction:
- Original: Disputes between States / Centre-State (Article 131)
- Appellate: Appeals from High Courts (Articles 132, 133, 134)
- Advisory: Presidential reference under Article 143
- Writ: Under Article 32 for enforcement of Fundamental Rights
- Special Leave Petition: Under Article 136

Services:
- Case filing: https://www.sci.gov.in
- Cause list: https://www.sci.gov.in/causelist
- Judgments: https://www.sci.gov.in/judgments
- Helpline: 011-23388942 / 23388943

=== NALSA (NATIONAL LEGAL SERVICES AUTHORITY) ===

NALSA was constituted under the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 to provide free legal aid and access to justice for the weaker sections of society.

Who is eligible for free legal aid:
- Members of SC/ST communities
- Women and children
- Persons with disabilities
- Industrial workmen
- Persons in custody
- Victims of mass disaster, ethnic violence, caste atrocity, flood, drought, earthquake or industrial disaster
- Persons whose annual income does not exceed Rs. 3 lakh (in Supreme Court Legal Aid) or as specified by the State

NALSA services:
- Free legal representation in courts
- Legal literacy and awareness programs
- Lok Adalats for alternative dispute resolution
- Victim compensation schemes
- Pre-litigation counselling

NALSA Helpline: 15100
Website: https://nalsa.gov.in
